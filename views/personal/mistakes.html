We are all human, and we all make mistakes. Especially when emotions get the better of us. However, our moderators should be able to own their mistakes and handle such situations with dignity and grace. If you make an error that negatively impacts a user, it is very important that you correct the error as soon as possible.

However, we do not want the user to go on a rampage publicly about what happened before we've had a chance to talk with them. To prevent escalating the situation even further, it is best to have another moderator present to mediate the situation. Even if you just want to apologise for what happened, the user might still be angry and lash out at you again. This is also why you absolutely want to avoid talking privately with the user. It prevents "He said / She said" situations where no one really knows what actually happened as they weren't present during the conversation.

Lastly it is very important that you do apologise directly to the user and let them know that they didn't do anything that warranted a ban. Ensure them that there will be no records against the user where disciplinary action was taken against them.
