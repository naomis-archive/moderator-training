An important thing to remember is that we are a team and we must present a united front to our users. It is okay to disagree with the way a fellow moderator handled a situation - disagreements are inevitable sometimes. However, you want to handle the situation privately and with both tact and grace, and you never want to approach another moderator in an aggressive manner.

Sending the moderator a message expressing your concern and asking for their thoughts behind the decision is an excellent approach and can allow you to see things from another perspective. However, if you and the other moderator are unable to come to an agreement, you can always reach out to a third moderator (such as Bjorno) to help mediate the situation.

Expressing your concerns in the private moderator chat can be a viable option - however, if this is the route you choose to take it is important to present your thoughts as a desire to learn, rather than an accusation of malice or incompetence.

Our top priority is to create an open and welcoming environment for all of our users, and that includes our fellow moderators. And NEVER disagree with a moderator in public. Again, we want to present a unified front toward our users.
